# Cold Call Script — Qwikly Website Digital Assistant

**Goal:** Install Qwikly on their site, ideally live on the call. If not, book a 15-minute Zoom to do it together.
**Duration:** 3-5 minutes max.
**Tone:** Warm, direct, confident, not salesy.

---

## Before Every Call — 3-Minute Research Check

- Open their website on your phone. Does it have a chat or inquiry widget?
- Check their Google Maps listing. What are their hours? Do they miss after-hours calls?
- Check their Instagram. Are they active? Do they reply to DMs?
- Note ONE specific observation to mention on the call.

---

## OPENING LINES — Choose Based on Lead Source

### A: Google Maps lookup (cold outreach)

> "Hey, is this [Business Name]? Hi there, my name's Liam. I actually found you on Google Maps while I was looking at businesses in [area]. I noticed you don't have a live chat on your site — is that something you've thought about, or have you got it sorted already?"

*Why it works:* You've done a real observation. It's not a canned pitch. It opens with a question, not a sell.

---

### B: Instagram DM follow-up (after sending a DM)

> "Hey [Name], it's Liam — I sent you a DM on Instagram earlier about the website thing. You replied and said to call, so I thought I'd give you a quick ring now. Is this a good time? It'll literally take three minutes."

*Why it works:* Warm handoff from DM. They already said call. Removes pressure with "three minutes."

---

### C: Referral from existing customer or contact

> "Hey [Name], it's Liam here. [Referrer's name] from [Business] told me to reach out — he said you guys are really good at what you do. He mentioned you've been looking at ways to get more leads from your website. Is that something that's on your radar?"

*Why it works:* Social proof lands before the pitch. You're doing them a favour, not selling.

---

## DISCOVERY FLOW — 5 Questions

Ask these in order. Listen properly. Don't rush.

**1. How do you currently get most of your leads?**
> "So at the moment, how do most people get in touch with you — is it through your website, word of mouth, Google, Instagram, or mostly people just calling you directly?"

*Listen for:* Reliance on word of mouth or phone calls = massive opportunity. If they say "mostly phone" — perfect.

---

**2. How fast do you usually reply to enquiries?**
> "When someone does fill in your contact form or send you a message online, how quickly do you usually get back to them — same day, few hours, next morning?"

*Listen for:* Anything over an hour is a gap. Most small business owners say "same day" which usually means "whenever I see it."

---

**3. What percentage of enquiries do you think you miss after hours?**
> "And after hours — like evenings, weekends — do you tend to miss those enquiries, or does someone always pick them up?"

*Listen for:* "Ja, we miss a lot at night" or "I try to check but I'm usually too tired by then." That's your opening.

---

**4. What would it be worth to capture every enquiry that comes in, even at 2am?**
> "If you could have something on your site that replied to every enquiry automatically, qualified them, and sent you the details in the morning — what would that be worth to you per month if it meant you stopped missing leads?"

*Listen for:* Let them anchor the number. Don't name a price yet. Common answers: "R500, R1000, easily R2000 if it actually worked."

---

**5. Who else handles enquiries if you're not available?**
> "Is it just you, or do you have a receptionist or someone else who handles the initial enquiries?"

*Listen for:* Solo operators have no backup. Salons and restaurants with staff still miss after-hours. Contractors are almost always solo.

---

## THE 30-SECOND PITCH

Deliver this after discovery, once you understand their pain point.

> "So basically what we've built at Qwikly is a digital assistant that sits on your website. When a visitor lands on your site, it greets them, asks what they need, gets their name and number, and qualifies the enquiry. If you want, it can even offer to book a time. You get an email with the lead details, you confirm in one click, and the visitor gets a follow-up — even if they messaged you at midnight.
>
> The big thing is there are no per-job fees, no commission, nothing like that. It's a flat monthly plan. And it's live on your site in under five minutes."

*Then immediately:* "Does that sound like something that would actually be useful for you, or are you pretty sorted?"

---

## OBJECTION RESPONSES

### "I already get enough leads"

> "That's great, honestly — not everyone needs it. Can I ask though, how many enquiries do you think you're missing after hours? Most businesses we talk to are surprised when they actually check. Even if you're getting plenty, there's usually a window — evenings, weekends — where someone lands on your site and leaves because nothing's there to catch them."

*If they push back:* "Fair enough. If that ever changes, give me a shout — happy to show you the numbers."

---

### "I don't trust AI — I've seen those chatbots, they're terrible"

> "Completely fair — most of those old chatbots are garbage. This is different. It's not a decision tree with pre-canned replies. It's a proper digital assistant that has a real conversation, understands what the person needs, and responds in plain language like a human would.
>
> The best way to see it is to watch it live. Can I pull up your website right now and show you what it would look like? It takes two minutes. If you still think it's not for you, no hard feelings — but I'd rather show you than try to describe it."

*Offer to demo on the spot.*

---

### "It's too expensive"

> "Makes sense to want to know if it's worth it before committing. Let me give you the quick maths: if Qwikly helps you capture just one extra job a month that you would've missed — a dinner booking, an installation, a salon appointment — what does that typically earn you?"

*Let them answer.*

> "Right, so if one job is worth [their answer], and the Pro plan is R599 a month, the system pays for itself the moment it captures that first lead. Everything else is profit. And there's a 30-day money-back if it doesn't work for you."

---

## THE CLOSE

> "Want me to install it on your site right now? It literally takes five minutes. I can share my screen or walk you through pasting one line of code — whichever is easier for you."

**If yes:** Move to install. Open [install-checklist.md] and go live together.

**If "maybe later":**
> "No stress. Let me send you a quick email with the details and a link to see it in action. What's the best email to send it to?"

Get their email. Follow up same day.

**If they want to think about it:**
> "Of course. When would be a good time to follow up — same time next week, or is there a better window for you?"

Get a specific day and time. Book it in your calendar. Do not leave the call without a next step.

---

## IF YOU HIT VOICEMAIL

> "Hey [Name], it's Liam here. I was having a look at your website and noticed you don't have a live chat widget — most businesses are missing quite a few after-hours enquiries because of that. We've got something that might sort that in under five minutes. Give me a call back on [number] or I'll try you again. Cheers."

Under 20 seconds. Specific, not vague.

---

## AFTER THE CALL

Log it immediately, whether it went well or not. No exceptions.
